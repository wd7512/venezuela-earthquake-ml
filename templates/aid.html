Where to Get Aid

Distribution points, shelters, and medical services. If you're in an affected area, this page tells you where to go right now.

If this is a medical emergency, call 911 (Venezuela). For the Venezuelan Red Cross, dial their local chapter or visit cruzrojana.org.ve.

Emergency Contacts

  • Emergency services: 911 (Venezuela)
  • Venezuelan Red Cross: Local chapters coordinating shelters and medical care
  • UN OCHA Venezuela: Humanitarian coordination
  • PAHO/WHO: Health emergency response
  • IFRC: International Federation of Red Cross

Find Missing People

If someone you know is missing, search and post on these volunteer-run platforms (no login required):

  • Desaparecidos Terremoto Venezuela — Search and post missing persons with photos
  • Venezuela Te Busca — Report name, age, gender, location; searchable registry
  • Google Person Finder — Search or register missing persons
  • ICRC Restoring Family Links — Cross-border family tracing

Post to both volunteer platforms simultaneously for maximum reach. Never publish personal phone numbers.

Safety

Aftershock risk: {{ stats.aftershock_prob_mw6.value }}% chance of a strong aftershock within the next week. Do not enter visibly damaged structures. Avoid hillside areas (landslide risk).

What to Do

  • Drop, Cover, Hold On during aftershocks — get under a sturdy table or desk
  • Stay away from damaged buildings, overpasses, and slopes
  • Check on neighbours — especially elderly and children
  • Use text messages instead of calls to reduce network congestion
  • Charge devices now — widespread power outages reported
  • Drink bottled or boiled water until supply is confirmed safe
