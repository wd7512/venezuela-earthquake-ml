Situation Summary

On Wednesday, 24 June 2026 at 18:04 VET (22:04 UTC), Venezuela was struck by one of the most catastrophic seismic sequences in South American history — a rare doublet earthquake with two near-equal-magnitude quakes just 39 seconds apart.

EventMagnitudeDepthEpicentreTime (VET)
ForeshockMw 7.220.3 km~23 km SE of Yumare, Yaracuy18:04
MainshockMw 7.510.0 kmYumare–Morón area, Yaracuy18:04 + 39s
AftershockMw 4.9—61 km NW of Maracay26 Jun

Both epicentres in the Veroes municipality, Yaracuy state, ~284–293 km west of Caracas, along the San Sebastián fault system — boundary between Caribbean and South American plates. Shallow depth (10 km) dramatically amplified surface destruction. USGS classified this as the largest earthquake in Venezuela since the 1900 San Narciso event (Mw 7.7).

Tectonic Context

  • San Sebastián fault system — right-lateral strike-slip at Caribbean/South American plate boundary
  • Plates moving at ~20 mm/yr · Fault extends ~400 km offshore
  • Same system caused major quakes in 1641, 1766, 1812, 1900, 1967
  • Doublet structure (two near-equal events 39s apart) is scientifically rare
  • Slip on main rupture reached 2 metres west of Puerto Cruz — zone of maximum damage

Media & Communications Workarounds

Venezuela's government maintains one of the most restrictive internet environments in Latin America.

  • VPNs: Tunnelbear, Proton VPN, or Psiphon work on mobile data. Share download links via SMS before going online.
  • Tor Browser: torproject.org — Works in heavily censored environments
  • VE Sin Filtro: vesinfiltro.com — Tracks which sites are blocked in real-time
  • WhatsApp: Generally functional even with restrictions; primary communication tool inside Venezuela
  • SMS: More reliable than data connections for reaching people in affected areas
