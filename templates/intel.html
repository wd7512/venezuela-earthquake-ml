Monitoring Resources

Comprehensive reference for real-time earthquake monitoring, missing persons, damage mapping, and humanitarian tracking.

Official Seismic Monitoring

  • National FUNVISIS — Primary national authority: bulletins, epicentres, magnitude/depth, public advisories
  • Global USGS Earthquake Hazards — Real-time feeds, ShakeMap, PAGER impact estimates, aftershock sequences
  • Europe EMSC — Rapid notifications, crowd-sourced felt reports
  • Science GEOFON — Scientific catalogues, seismic waveforms, regional monitoring
  • Caribbean UWI Seismic Research Centre — Regional Caribbean monitoring

Emergency & Missing Persons

  • Red Cross ICRC Family Links — Family reunification and missing persons tracing
  • UN IFRC — Situation reports and humanitarian coordination
  • Government Protección Civil Venezuela — State emergency agencies, municipal governments
  • Health Ministry of Health announcements, local hospitals, emergency operations centres

Damage Mapping & Satellite

  • Satellite Copernicus EMS — Building damage, road accessibility, flood/landslide mapping
  • Satellite UNOSAT — Satellite-derived damage assessments
  • Imagery NASA Worldview — Near real-time satellite imagery
  • Imagery NASA FIRMS — Fire tracking post-earthquake
  • Mapping HOT-OSM — Community-updated infrastructure maps

Humanitarian Reports

  • ReliefWeb · OCHA · WHO
  • UNICEF · WFP · IOM
  • Typical info: deaths, injuries, missing, displaced, shelter, medical capacity, infrastructure damage

Dangerous Areas to Monitor

  • ShakeMap intensity zones · Landslide risk · Liquefaction zones
  • Bridge failures · Damaged hospitals · Gas leaks · Building collapse zones
  • Aftershock forecasts — monitor for several weeks after main event
  • Never enter visibly damaged structures without official clearance

Logistics

  • FlightRadar24 · MarineTraffic · Logistics Cluster

News Sources

  • Reuters · Associated Press · BBC · Local Venezuelan media
  • Avoid relying solely on social media — verify with at least two independent sources

Monitoring Workflow

  1. Monitor FUNVISIS and USGS continuously
  2. Review aftershock forecasts
  3. Examine ShakeMap and damage products
  4. Check Copernicus and UNOSAT imagery
  5. Read ReliefWeb and OCHA daily reports
  6. Use Red Cross family-link services for missing persons
  7. Verify breaking news with multiple trusted sources
  8. Cross-check social media against official information
