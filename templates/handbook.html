Response Handbook

What to do during and after the earthquake. For residents, families, and volunteers.

Aftershock risk: {{ stats.aftershock_prob_mw6.value }}% chance of Mw 6+ within the next week ({{ stats.aftershock_prob_mw6.source }}). Do not enter damaged buildings. Stay away from slopes and weakened structures.

During an Aftershock

  • Drop, Cover, Hold On — get under a sturdy table or desk
  • If outdoors, move away from buildings, overpasses, power lines
  • If driving, pull over in an open area, stay inside
  • Do not run outside during shaking
  • Expect aftershocks — they can be nearly as strong as the mainshock

Immediately After

  • Check yourself and others for injuries — do not move seriously injured people
  • Wear shoes — broken glass and debris on the ground
  • Check for gas leaks (smell, hissing sound) — shut off gas if in doubt
  • Do not use elevators — use stairs only
  • Avoid damaged buildings, hillside areas, and riverbanks
  • If near the coast, move to high ground (tsunami risk) — no tsunami warning was issued for this event (inland strike-slip fault). This is general earthquake guidance.

Ongoing Hazards

What to Watch For

  • Aftershocks: Can continue for weeks. Each one weakens damaged structures further
  • Landslides: Active risk in northern coastal mountains, especially after rain
  • Liquefaction: Saturated soil behaves like liquid — buildings sink or tilt
  • Fires: Broken gas lines — report any gas smell immediately
  • Flooding: Dam damage and blocked drainage
  • Disease: Contaminated water, disrupted sanitation — boil water

Infrastructure Status

  • Simón Bolívar Airport (CCS): CLOSED — structural roof damage
  • Hospitals: {{ stats.hospitals_collapsed.value }} collapsed, {{ stats.hospitals_damaged.value }} damaged. Patients treated outdoors. Critical shortages of supplies
  • Mobile networks: Severely congested — use text, not calls
  • Power: Widespread outages in northern states
  • Internet: 200+ sites blocked. WhatsApp generally functional

Staying Connected

Venezuela has one of the most restricted internet environments. Here's how to work around it:

  • VPNs: Tunnelbear, Proton VPN, or Psiphon work on mobile data. Share links via SMS before going online
  • Tor Browser: torproject.org — Works in heavily censored environments
  • VE Sin Filtro: vesinfiltro.com — Tracks blocked sites in real-time
  • WhatsApp: Primary communication tool inside Venezuela
  • SMS: More reliable than data in affected areas
