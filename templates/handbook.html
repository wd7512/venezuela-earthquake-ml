Humanitarian Response Handbook

Structured approach to monitoring earthquake impacts in Venezuela. For volunteers, researchers, journalists, humanitarian responders, and OSINT practitioners. Emphasizes verification, safety, and authoritative sources.

Safety first: Never enter visibly damaged structures without official clearance. Cross-check every important claim. Respect privacy and dignity.

1. Situation Awareness

Monitor information in layers. Never rely on a single source.

  1. Earthquake detection
  2. Aftershock evolution
  3. Damage assessment
  4. Humanitarian impact
  5. Infrastructure status
  6. Logistics
  7. Official response
  8. Recovery

2. Seismic Monitoring

Official Sources

  • National FUNVISIS — Primary Venezuelan seismic authority
  • Global USGS — Real-time feeds, ShakeMap, PAGER estimates
  • Europe EMSC — Rapid notifications, crowd-sourced felt reports
  • Science GEOFON — Scientific catalogues, waveforms
  • Caribbean UWI Seismic Research Centre — Regional monitoring
  • Research IRIS — Historical analysis, earthquake browser

Track

  • Magnitude · Depth · Epicentre · Moment tensor
  • ShakeMap · PAGER estimates · Aftershock sequences

3. Humanitarian Information

Daily Sources

  • ReliefWeb · OCHA · IFRC
  • ICRC · WHO · UNICEF
  • WFP · IOM

Monitor

  • Fatalities · Injuries · Displaced persons · Shelter occupancy
  • Food security · Potable water · Sanitation · Electricity · Communications

4. Missing Persons

  • Red Cross ICRC Restoring Family Links — Family reunification
  • Local Venezuelan Red Cross · Hospitals · Municipal emergency centres

Recommended Workflow

  1. Record full name
  2. Record last known location and time
  3. Record reporting contact
  4. Verify before publishing
  5. Respect privacy — never publish personal phone numbers or sensitive medical information

5. Hazard Mapping

  • USGS ShakeMap · Copernicus EMS · UNOSAT
  • NASA Worldview · NASA FIRMS · HOT-OSM

Monitor: Landslides · Liquefaction · Fires · Flooding · Dam failures · Road closures · Bridge failures

6. Infrastructure Monitoring

  • Airports · Ports · Highways · Fuel
  • Hospitals · Schools · Cell towers · Internet outages
  • Power grid · Water treatment

7. Weather & Secondary Hazards

  • Heavy rainfall · Landslides · River flooding
  • Heat stress · Disease outbreaks

Monitor local forecasts continuously.

8. Health Resources

  • Hospital capacity · Trauma centres · Blood shortages
  • Disease surveillance · Water quality · Mental health services

9. OSINT Verification

Verification Checklist

  • Reverse image search
  • Confirm upload date
  • Confirm location using landmarks
  • Compare with satellite imagery
  • Compare shadows and weather
  • Check multiple eyewitnesses
  • Confirm with official agencies

Confidence Labels

Confirmed Likely Unconfirmed False

Maintain an audit trail for each assessment.
